1. The Agony

Bloody Sweat

After the Last Supper, Jesus goes to the Garden of Gethsemane. He separates to pray.

Faced with the weight of human sin, Jesus felt intense sorrow and fear. His sweat became like drops of blood (Luke 22:44).

This internal agony was the first sacrifice of the Passion.

The Cup

‘Father, let this cup pass if possible’ (Mt 26:39)

His disciples fall asleep

‘Father, not My will, but Yours be done.’

Life Lesson

Pray in your hardest moments

It’s okay to feel weak

Choose God’s will anyway

2. The Betrayal

The Kiss

Judas identifies Jesus with a kiss (Mt 26:47–50)

Betrayed by a friend for 30 pieces of silver. The pain of betrayal marks the beginning of His outward suffering.

Binding

The Hands that created the universe were bound with ropes.

He allowed Himself to be tied so that we could be set free from sin.

Life Lesson

Sometimes, the people we trust will hurt us.

Respond with faith, not vengeance.

Hold onto God, Even betrayal becomes a stage for salvation.

3. The High Priests

The Silence

The High Priests and the Sanhedrin question Jesus about His disciples (Mt 26:57–68).

Jesus remained silent.

He didn’t defend Himself or justify His actions.

Spitting

The very face that the Seraphim dare not look upon was covered in the spit of His creation.

He bore the deepest shame to restore our dignity.

The Slap

Struck across the face by the servant of the High Priest.

The Hand that fashioned Adam from the dust was struck by the hand of a descendant of Adam.

4. Peter's Denial

The Look

As the rooster crowed, Jesus looked at Peter. It was a look of deep sorrow and mercy that led to Peter's repentance.

Repentance

Peter went out and wept bitterly. He realized that while human strength fails, Christ's grace remains sufficient.

5. The Roman Trial

Scourge

Jesus is brought before Pontius Pilate, the Roman governor.

Pilate finds no fault in Him, yet sends Him to Herod—and then back again.

The crowd is given a choice: Jesus… or Barabbas.

Pilate washed his hands of the guilt of His death.

Scourge

6,666 lashes tore His sacred flesh.

He took these stripes so that by His wounds we might find eternal healing.

Akli-le-Soh

A crown of 72 thorns placed on His head.

He was literally wearing the curse of humanity on His head to remove it from us.

6. The Way to Karanio

Falls

Christ forced to carry His cross to Golgotha

Christ falls under the weight of the cross.

He was determined to fulfill His Father's will.

Simon of Cyrene

Simon of Cyrene helps carry the cross of God. (Mk 15:21)

We are reminded that we are called to bear one another's burdens.

Symbol of service and discipleship

7. The Crucifixion

Nails

They stretched him upon the cross.

Nails driven into his hands and feet.

The cross is raised and dropped into the rocky ground.

The Seven Words

“Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do.” (Luke 23:34)

“Truly, I say to you, today you will be with me in Paradise.” (Luke 23:43)

“Woman, behold your son. … Behold your mother.” (John 19:26-27)

“My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?” (Matthew 27:46 / Psalm 22:1)

“I thirst.” (John 19:28)

“Father, into your hands I commit my spirit.” (Luke 23:46)

“It is finished.” (John 19:30)

The Spear

His side is pierced, and Blood and Water flow.

The fountain of the Sacraments and the life of the Church.

Christ’s death is complete, fulfilling God’s plan for humanity.
