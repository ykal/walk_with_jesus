1. The Agony

Bloody Sweat

Faced with the weight of human sin, His sweat became like drops of blood. This internal agony was the first sacrifice of the Passion.

The Cup

"Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from Me." Jesus surrenders His human will to the divine plan for our salvation.

2. The Betrayal

The Kiss

Judas betrays the Son of Man with a kiss. The pain of betrayal from a friend marks the beginning of His outward suffering.

Te-asro (Binding)

The Hands that created the universe were bound with ropes. He allowed Himself to be tied so that we could be set free from sin.

3. The High Priests

Tazif (Spitting)

They spat on the face of the One who gives sight to the blind. He bore the deepest shame to restore our dignity.

Sama (The Slap)

Struck across the face by the servant of the High Priest. The Creator remained silent while the creation struck Him.

4. Peter's Denial

The Look

As the rooster crowed, Jesus looked at Peter. It was a look of deep sorrow and mercy that led to Peter's repentance.

Repentance

Peter went out and wept bitterly. He realized that while human strength fails, Christ's grace remains sufficient.

5. The Roman Trial

Meqwet (Scourge)

6,666 lashes tore His sacred flesh. He took these stripes so that by His wounds we might find eternal healing.

Akli-le-Soh

A crown of 72 thorns. He wore the curse of the earth to give us the crown of the Heavenly Kingdom.

6. The Way to Karanio

Wediqo (Falls)

Christ falls under the weight of the cross. He falls to the earth to lift us up from our own spiritual falls.

Simon of Cyrene

A human helps carry the cross of God. We are reminded that we are called to bear one another's burdens.

7. The Sacrifice

Mismari (Nails)

Iron through the hands that healed and the feet that brought peace. He is fixed to the wood to anchor our salvation.

Qanat (The Spear)

His side is pierced, and Blood and Water flow—the fountain of the Sacraments and the life of the Church.

8. The Seven Words

Forgiveness

"Father, forgive them." In the height of His pain, Christ’s first priority is interceding for those who caused it.

Victory

"It is finished." The mission of redemption is complete. The gates of Paradise are no longer barred.

9. Reflection

Your Journey

The 13 Sufferings were a personal act of love for you. He took the pain so you could have the peace. How will you respond today?
